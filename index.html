Critical Operations & Solutions

Challenge: Difficult to attach top and bottom cups due to curve shape

Solution: Stopped stitching periodically to lift pressure foot and turn fabric according to shape

Challenge: Loose fit in bust seam area (3mm at bust point)

Solution: Reduced curve of bust seam line in patterns

Challenge: Wrong grain direction in first trial

Solution: Implemented strict fabric cutting direction checks

Challenge: Foamed zig zag stitches not in continuous line

Solution: Improved handling skills for feeding both top and bottom cups simultaneously

Fit and Quality Issues in Final Garment

Issue 1: Unable to bar tack at top and bottom of side seam covering of bra underwire

Solution: Reduce whalebone length to create space for bar tacking

Issue 2: Gatherings around the bra cup near to side seam

Solution: Use paper when stitching to avoid gathers and ensure matching measurements

Issue 3: Loose fit at arm hole

Solution: Adjust patterns and feed elastic more tightly during attachment

Issue 4: Uneven seam width at side seam

Solution: Improve handling skills and back tack before continuous stitching

Issue 5: Uneven seam width at hook and eye attaching

Solution: Back tack before attaching hook and eye

Issue 6: Unable to bar tack at start and end of bra underwire

Solution: Check pattern measurements and increase around cup length

Issue 7: Strap not attached to bar tack properly, wing tends to curl

Solution: Back tack before bar tacking and check stitch formation

Issue 8: Gathers at center front

Solution: Use paper while attaching stabilizer and check measurements

Hydrophobic Finishes & Plasma Technology

Final Year Research Project

Investigated the impact of hydrophobic finishes on adhesive bonding of knitted fabrics and explored plasma technology to enhance bondability.

  • Analyzed key parameters affecting fabric bondability
  • Tested seam strength and durability
  • Developed recommendations for garment construction

Research Project Details

Research Objectives
  • Investigate impact of hydrophobic finishes on adhesive bonding
  • Analyze parameters affecting fabric bondability
  • Explore plasma technology to improve bondability
  • Evaluate seam strength and durability
Methodology
  1. Prepared fabric samples with different hydrophobic treatments
  2. Conducted bond strength tests using standard methods
  3. Applied plasma treatment to selected samples
  4. Performed comparative analysis of bond strength
  5. Evaluated durability through repeated wash tests
Key Findings
  • Hydrophobic finishes reduced initial bond strength by 15-20%
  • Plasma treatment improved bond strength by 25-30%
  • Optimal plasma treatment parameters identified
  • Combination treatments showed best durability results
Conclusions

The research demonstrated that plasma technology can effectively counteract the negative effects of hydrophobic finishes on fabric bondability, leading to improved seam strength and garment durability in intimate wear applications.
